# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful community for all members, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior Examples

We encourage and appreciate:

- **Respectful Communication** - Listen actively, communicate clearly, and assume good intent
- **Constructive Feedback** - Provide helpful, actionable feedback in a kind manner
- **Inclusivity** - Welcome people of all backgrounds and experience levels
- **Patience** - Remember everyone is volunteering their time
- **Collaboration** - Work together toward common goals
- **Gratitude** - Thank contributors and acknowledge their efforts
- **Safety** - Create a space where everyone feels safe and valued

---

## Unacceptable Behavior

**We do not tolerate:**

### Harassment
- Sexual harassment or unwanted sexual attention
- Personal attacks or insults
- Intimidation or threats
- Stalking or following

### Discrimination
- Discrimination based on protected characteristics
- Exclusion based on personal attributes
- Derogatory language or slurs
- Bias or prejudiced behavior

### Disruptive Conduct
- Trolling or intentionally disruptive comments
- Spam or excessive self-promotion
- Off-topic posts in issue discussions
- Derailing conversations

### Other Misconduct
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Abuse of the issue/PR system
- Deliberately sharing malicious code
- Violating others' intellectual property rights

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- **GitHub Issues** - All discussions and comments
- **Pull Requests** - All feedback and reviews
- **Discussions** - Community conversations
- **Related Communications** - Email, chat, social media discussions about the project

---

## Enforcement

### Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness a Code of Conduct violation:

1. **Do not engage** in argument with the person
2. **Document the incident** with details and screenshots
3. **Report privately** to project maintainers
4. **Be specific** about what happened and why it violated the Code

### Consequences

Violations may result in:

- **Warning** - Private discussion about the behavior
- **Comment Removal** - Deletion of inappropriate posts
- **Temporary Suspension** - Temporary loss of contribution privileges
- **Permanent Ban** - Removal from the community

The severity of action depends on the nature and context of the violation.

---

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- **Clarifying Standards** - Explaining what constitutes acceptable behavior
- **Consistent Enforcement** - Applying the Code of Conduct fairly
- **Fair Process** - Giving people opportunity to address concerns
- **Transparency** - Explaining decisions when appropriate
- **Privacy** - Keeping reports confidential

---

## Appeals

If you believe enforcement action was unjust:

1. Request a private discussion with maintainers
2. Explain your perspective respectfully
3. Provide any additional context
4. Accept the maintainers' final decision

---

## Community Guidelines by Context

### Issue Discussions

- **Stay on topic** - Keep discussions relevant to the issue
- **Be constructive** - Suggest solutions, not just criticisms
- **Respect expertise** - Recognize others' knowledge and experience
- **No spam** - Don't promote unrelated products or services
- **Search first** - Check if similar issues exist

### Pull Requests

- **Respectful reviews** - Critique code, not the person
- **Acknowledge effort** - Thank contributors for their work
- **Be patient** - Allow time for responses and revisions
- **Constructive suggestions** - Offer alternative approaches
- **Focus on quality** - Help improve the contribution

### Feature Requests

- **Be clear** - Explain why you need the feature
- **Be realistic** - Consider project scope and resources
- **Offer to help** - Volunteer to contribute if interested
- **Respect decisions** - Accept if a feature isn't accepted
